My research focuses on the development of advanced **computational and
numerical methods** for the simulation of **single-** and
**multiphase flows**, with applications spanning mechanical engineering,
nuclear engineering, chemical processes, biomechanics, porous media,
microfluidics, and environmental sciences. My work combines
**computational fluid dynamics (CFD)**, **scientific computing**, and
the **Finite Element Method (FEM)** to investigate highly coupled
multiphysics phenomena involving fluid motion, deformable interfaces,
heat and mass transfer, fluid-structure interaction, and complex
transport processes.

I am currently a **Professor** at the
`Department of Mechanical Engineering`_ /
`COPPE`_ / `Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ)`_,
where I lead research activities in advanced numerical methods for
multiphysics and multiphase flows. I also serve as the **Head** of the
Laboratory of Fluid Mechanics and Aerodynamics (`LabMFA`_). My research
group develops high-fidelity computational methodologies for complex
fluid systems involving moving interfaces, nonlinear couplings,
deformable structures, and heat and mass transfer.

Over the years, my research has concentrated on the formulation and
implementation of robust numerical frameworks based on the
**Arbitrary Lagrangian-Eulerian (ALE)** description, moving mesh
techniques, front-tracking methods, immersed finite element methods,
and semi-Lagrangian formulations for incompressible flows. Particular
attention has been devoted to the numerical treatment of
**interfacial dynamics**, surface tension effects, phase change,
multiscale transport, and strongly nonlinear coupled systems.

I have developed several in-house high-performance numerical solvers
using modern object-oriented programming techniques in `C++`_ and
`Python`_, enabling flexible implementations for large-scale and
multiphysics simulations. My research also includes the development of
new computational strategies for:

 - two-phase and free-surface flows,
 - fluid-structure interaction (FSI),
 - non-Newtonian flows,
 - heat and mass transfer,
 - porous and Darcy-Forchheimer flows,
 - immersed and decoupled mesh methods,
 - front-tracking and moving interface techniques,
 - nonlinear variational formulations,
 - particle-laden and biofluid simulations.

During my doctoral studies at `EPFL`_, I focused on the numerical
discretization of fluid motion equations and the modeling of
**interfacial forces** within finite element frameworks for multiphase
flows. My Ph.D. thesis [`link`_] was selected among the
**top 5-10% best theses** written at `EPFL`_ in 2012.

From August 2012 to August 2013, I worked as a Postdoctoral Associate
in Nuclear Science & Engineering (`NSE`_) at the Massachusetts
Institute of Technology (`MIT`_) in Cambridge/Boston. The objective of
this position was to benchmark experimental databases of
**boiling and condensation phenomena** using advanced interface-capturing
numerical methodologies. In September 2013, I returned to Brazil as a
Postdoctoral Fellow sponsored by CAPES/Science Without Borders -
Young Talent Fellowship.

In 2014, I joined the Mechanical Engineering Department at `UERJ`_ as
a young professor. Since 2019, I have held a faculty position at
`UFRJ`_/`COPPE`_/`PEM`_, where I currently supervise research projects
in computational mechanics, multiphase flows, and scientific computing.
I am also a Young Researcher of the State of Rio de Janeiro
(`FAPERJ`_) and a recipient of the Royal Society-Newton Advanced
Fellowship (`more info`_).
